SAMOSA RECIPE

A samosa is a popular, deep-fried snack originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is a triangular pastry filled with a savory mixture, typically including spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes lentils or meat. The crispy, golden outer layer contrasts with the soft, flavorful filling, making it a favorite street food and appetizer. Samosas are often served with chutneys, like mint or tamarind, enhancing their taste with a balance of sweet, tangy, and spicy flavors. Over time, the samosa has gained popularity globally, with various regional adaptations.

Ingredients

  • For the dough(outer crust)
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
    • 1/4 teaspoon ghee
    • water
  • For the filling
    • 3-4 medium potatoes(boiled,ppeled and mashed)
    • 1/2 cup green peas
    • 1 small onion
    • 1 tablespoon cumin,coriander seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, amchur
    • Fresh coriander leaves(chopped)
    • salt to taste
    • Oil
